About my poetry

My poetry is an expression of who I am and where I’ve come from. Life can be dark and terrifying when you have schizophrenia, but there is always a light at the end of the tunnel if you look hard enough. Spring is a metaphor for my hope, this is my green September.

  • Schizophrenia

    Cold festering worms of the mind
    Darkness, confusion
    Fitful sleep, voices in the night
    Terror, terror

    Loneliness, castration
    Fear, delusion
    Frightful beasts in dark corners
    Spiders, rats and mice
    Scurrying in the undergrowth

    Fear, fear
    Mortal terror

  • Hopeful Spring

    Out of darkness hope germinates,
    Bright and lustrous like spring flowers in the sun.
    As a ravenous winter fades,
    A warm dewy meadow bathes us in sunlight.

    Hope, the fading of a cold dolorous blue.
    Hope, abounding with new born vitality.
    Lush, moist, and verdant, the meadows of my hopeful spring.
    May the tender shoots of love emerge from these fertile soils.

  • Hope Series 1

    Clutching at strands of hope,
    A desperate forlorn lust.
    Pinned down by experience,
    Snivelling like a wounded animal.
    A joyless austere castration.

    But hope flourishes like bountiful spring flowers.
    A delicate vision of tender flesh.
    Hair like silk running through my fingers,
    Warm flesh like a caress,
    A sensual dream of freedom.

  • Hope Series 2

    My hope is a strange creature, fragile and frightened,
    Startled and scattered at the slightest sound.
    Nowhere to be found in an ocean of humanity,
    But still present here and there, now and then,
    Ever caged in mortal terror.

    My hope, alive and vivid,
    In sweet dreams, drifting off to sleep beneath a heavy rain.
    My hope a pluviophilic blessing.
    My hope sometimes takes flight in warm reveries of brighter tomorrows;
    Else buried in multi-layered anxiety.

    Hope, how may I clasp you close like a partner in lust?

  • Wellington

    Wellington is;
    Fierce autumnal wind, Tousled hair.
    Apocalyptic gales, Chilled bones.

    Oh azure sky, oh winter murk.
    Oh whirling tempest, oh bitter breeze.
    This maelstrom is sacred!

    This place is holy.
    This howling beast, this bitter brew.
    Ayahusca for the soul.
    My home ever unsettled.

  • Palace of Dreams

    Lust is a palace of dreams.
    Humility is a tragedy here,
    Let the sensual animal within roam free.
    The wildest passions gleefully unfurled.

    She who drew long breath weeps warm rivulets,
    Writhing like a wounded animal, holy and supine.
    I’ll do anything to hear the sacred hymn of your beautiful agony.
    Only chastity is unholy in the palace of dreams.

The ideal experience

To set the tone and atmosphere for the experience of my art I recommend reading, listening and watching at night. My poetry is intended to be read by candle light if possible. Feel free to light some insence (I recommend Nag Champa) and pour yourself a glass of wine or take your drug of choice. Listen to the loops and songs while you read. Please enjoy!
