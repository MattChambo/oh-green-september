  • Hopeful Spring

    Out of darkness hope germinates, Bright and lustrous like spring flowers in the sun. As a ravenous winter fades, A warm dewy meadow bathes us in sunlight.

    Hope, the fading of a cold dolorous blue. Hope, abounding with new born vitality. Lush, moist, and verdant, the meadows of my hopeful spring. May the tender shoots of love emerge from these fertile soils.

  • Hope

    Clutching at strands of hope, A desperate forlorn lust. Pinned down by experience, Snivelling like a wounded animal. A joyless austere castration.

    But hope flourishes like bountiful spring flowers. A delicate vision of tender flesh. Hair like silk running through my fingers, Warm flesh like a caress, A sensual dream of freedom.

  • Hope Series 2

    My hope is a strange creature, fragile and frightened, Startled and scattered at the slightest sound. Nowhere to be found in an ocean of humanity, But still present here and there, now and then, Ever caged in mortal terror.

    My hope, alive and vivid, In sweet dreams, drifting off to sleep beneath a heavy rain. My hope a pluviophilic blessing. My hope sometimes takes flight in warm reveries of brighter tomorrows; Else buried in multi-layered anxiety.

    Hope, how may I clasp you close like a partner in lust?
